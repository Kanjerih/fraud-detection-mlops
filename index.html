Known limitations

Where this model can mislead you

! Severe class imbalance. Fraud is 0.17% of the data — accuracy is meaningless here; every metric on the Dashboard was chosen specifically to avoid that trap.
! Anonymized features. V1–V28 are PCA components with no real-world meaning available — the model can't be interrogated for *why* it flagged a transaction beyond feature importances.
! Static training snapshot. Trained on 2 days of transactions from one point in time — real fraud patterns shift, and this model has no mechanism to detect that drift on its own yet.
! Cost assumption is a placeholder. The $10-per-false-positive figure driving the recommended threshold was not sourced from a real business — it should be replaced before this logic informs any real decision.
! 1-in-5 fraud cases missed. At 78.5% recall, roughly 22% of actual fraud in any given batch would pass through undetected.
